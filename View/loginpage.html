Manga is a style of Japanese comics known for its expressive art and deep storytelling. It covers every genre—from action and fantasy to romance and slice of life. Read right to left, manga often builds rich worlds and emotional characters. For many, it’s not just reading—it’s an experience.

(One Piece by Eiichiro Oda)

Manhwa is Korean comics known for their clean art, full color pages, and vertical scroll format. They often feature strong storytelling in genres like action, fantasy, romance, and reincarnation. Most are made for digital reading

"I remembered the first time I was about to finish a book. It was like being deprived of the world."

(Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint by Sing Shong)

A light novel is a Japanese style of short novel aimed mainly at young adults. It usually includes anime-style illustrations and is written in simple, fast-paced language. Most light novels focus on genres like fantasy, adventure, romance , and school life.

"Phew, looks like I’ve managed to trick another person into becoming a member. No, I’ve managed to recruit another member…"

- Klein Moretti
( Lord of Mysteries by Yuan Ye)

Comics are a visual storytelling medium that combines illustrations and text to convey a narrative. They can range from superhero adventures to slice-of-life stories, often serialized in magazines or books. Comics use panels, speech bubbles, and sequential art to bring characters and worlds to life.
